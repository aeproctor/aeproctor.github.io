2020 Line-Up of Events

New Acquisitions Preview

Date: Sunday, January 19th

Time: 1:30PM

Location: Marshfield Ventress Public Library lecture room

Hear from Board Members Helen Demers, Cindy Castro, and Dottie Melcher about the Society's newest acquisitions. These include additions from the historic Ford family and photographic plates and photos donated by the Estate of Faith Jean of Brant Rock.

Attendes will also learn about the Society's Acquistions Fund and how we use it to purchase new artifacts related to Marshfield.

Pro-Am Lecture: Re-Imaging the Life of Penelope Winslow

Date: Sunday, February 16th

Time: 1:30PM

Location: Village at Proprietors Green auditorium

Suggested Donation: $5 for members; $10 for non-members

Author and historian Michelle Marchetti Coughlin presents on Penelope Winslow, the first lady of Plymouth Colony. Couglhin illuminates the largely forgotten story of one of the most powerful women of the Colony and subsequently reveals the lives of all women in her time.

Coughlin is the author of One Colonial Woman's World: The Life and Writings of Mehetabel Coit. Her most recent book on Penelope Winslow will be available for the purchase with the author's inscription.

2020 Annual Meeting of the Marshfield historical Society (POSTPONED)

Date: Sunday, March 22nd

Time: 1:00PM

Location: Haddad's Ocean Cafe second-floor banquet room

Learn about the restoration of the iron markers celebrating the shipyards on the North River of the 18th and 19th centuries from our featured speaker, Caleb Estabrooks.

Pro-Am Lecture Pilgrim Peregrine White's First Biography

Date: Sunday, November 15th

Time: TBD

Location: TBD

In celebration of Plymouth-400, the Board of the Marshhfield Historical Society voted unanimously to publish a monograph on the life of Peregrine White, the first Englishman to be born and reside in British Colonial America. The author, Stephen O'Neill, will give a lecture on the topic at a very special Pro-Am lecture.
